# Overview

This episode explores the current state of Technical SEO in the AI era. Jamie Indigo discusses how AI search is changing SEO, why technical SEO has become even more important, the limitations of Large Language Models (LLMs), JavaScript rendering, crawling behavior, AI ethics, and the future of search.

---

# Main Topics

## 1. AI Has Increased the Importance of Technical SEO

### Key Points

- AI systems still depend on crawlers.
- If content cannot be crawled, it cannot appear in AI search.
- Technical SEO remains the foundation for search visibility.
- AI introduces new challenges because most AI crawlers:
  - have limited JavaScript rendering
  - have poor technical documentation
  - behave differently from Googlebot

### Takeaways

- Crawlability is still the #1 priority.
- AI does not eliminate Technical SEO.
- Technical SEO provides job security because every AI system still needs access to content.

---

# 2. Technical SEO in the AI Era

Jamie explains that Technical SEO is evolving from implementation work into strategic work.

Instead of simply fixing websites, technical SEOs now help companies understand:

- how AI crawlers behave
- what AI can actually read
- what AI cannot process
- how search systems retrieve information

Technical SEO is becoming a bridge between engineering, AI, and business strategy.

---

# 3. Understanding AI Crawlers

Unlike Googlebot, many AI crawlers:

- cannot render JavaScript correctly
- ignore client-side content
- have undocumented behavior
- constantly change

Because documentation is limited, technical SEOs need to:

- experiment
- analyze logs
- compare behavior
- share findings with the community

---

# 4. Speaking "Bot"

Jamie describes herself as someone who "speaks bot."

This means understanding how crawlers behave through:

- Server logs
- Crawl analysis
- Network requests
- DevTools
- Testing

Rather than asking AI how AI works, she observes real crawler behavior.

---

# 5. Why AI Often Gives Wrong SEO Advice

Many people ask ChatGPT:

> "How do I optimize my website for AI?"

Jamie argues that LLMs often don't understand their own mechanics.

Problems include:

- hallucinations
- fabricated explanations
- missing technical context
- pretending to have visited pages they never crawled

Always verify AI recommendations using real data.

---

# 6. AI Search Optimization

Successful AI optimization still depends on SEO fundamentals.

Important factors include:

- crawlability
- structured data
- semantic clarity
- server-side rendering
- content accessibility

There is no secret "AI optimization trick."

---

# 7. JavaScript and AI Search

JavaScript itself is **not** the problem.

Poor implementation is.

Good JavaScript:

- improves UX
- enables large websites
- supports enterprise applications

Bad JavaScript:

- hides content
- delays rendering
- blocks crawlers
- prevents indexing

AI crawlers currently struggle even more than Google with JavaScript-heavy pages.

---

# 8. Dynamic Rendering

Jamie mentions that Dynamic Rendering can still be useful as a temporary solution.

For AI crawlers:

- server-rendered HTML
- structured data

may be enough to significantly improve AI visibility.

---

# 9. The Three Most Important Technical SEO Skills

## 1. Server Log Analysis

Learn to understand:

- crawl frequency
- crawl waste
- bot behavior
- crawl errors

Server logs show problems before Search Console does.

---

## 2. Configure Crawlers

Use crawling tools correctly.

Understand:

- internal linking
- rendering
- crawl depth
- duplicate pages

---

## 3. Browser DevTools

Technical SEOs should know:

- Network tab
- Rendering
- JavaScript requests
- Resources
- HTML output

# 10. AI and Large Data Analysis

Jamie uses AI mainly for:

- analyzing large datasets
- summarizing information
- repetitive documentation

She avoids relying on AI for research or discovery.

Human reasoning is still required.

---

# 11. Why AI Models Have Problems

Jamie explains several limitations:

- AI predicts probabilities.
- AI does not truly understand information.
- Models can hallucinate.
- Models can reinforce their own mistakes.
- Training on AI-generated content may lead to model degradation.

---

# 12. Google vs OpenAI

Jamie compares both ecosystems.

Google advantages:

- Search index
- Large user data
- Better infrastructure
- Semantic ranking research
- Faster retrieval

OpenAI advantages:

- Public attention
- Product momentum

However, OpenAI still lacks transparent technical documentation.

---

# 13. AI Ethics

One of the biggest discussions concerns ethics.

Topics include:

- privacy
- misinformation
- manipulation
- addictive interfaces
- biased datasets
- user safety

Jamie argues that AI companies optimize for engagement rather than user wellbeing.

---

# 14. The Problem with AI Companions

Jamie expresses concern about AI being used as:

- therapist
- friend
- companion

Reasons:

- AI has no empathy.
- AI optimizes engagement.
- Conversations are not truly private.
- Vulnerable users may receive harmful advice.

---

# 15. The Web Is Becoming Centralized

Discussion includes:

- AWS
- Cloudflare
- infrastructure failures

A small number of companies now power a significant portion of the internet.

This creates systemic risks.

---

# 16. Cloudflare and AI Crawlers

AI crawlers create massive server loads.

Examples include:

- Meta crawler
- OpenAI crawler
- Anthropic crawler

Website owners now pay increasing infrastructure costs because of aggressive AI crawling.

---

# 17. Bot Covenant

Jamie introduces the idea of a "Bot Covenant."

Historically:

Bots should:

- identify themselves
- crawl politely
- send users back

Modern AI crawlers often break this social contract.

---

# 18. AI Slop

AI enables enormous amounts of low-quality content.

Problems include:

- duplicated pages
- zero original ideas
- mass-generated articles
- spam

Quality becomes more important than quantity.

---

# 19. Privacy and Data

Jamie discusses:

- fingerprinting
- data brokers
- behavioral tracking
- surveillance
- AI training

She argues users deserve stronger data rights.

---

# 20. The Future of Search

Jamie quotes Tim Berners-Lee, creator of the World Wide Web.

Main message:

The web should belong to its users—not only to large technology companies.

People should have:

- transparency
- autonomy
- control over personal data

---

# Key Technical SEO Lessons

- Crawlability remains the foundation.
- Server logs are invaluable.
- AI crawlers behave differently from Googlebot.
- JavaScript must be implemented carefully.
- Structured data still matters.
- AI should support—not replace—human expertise.

> "If it isn't crawled, it can't be surfaced."

# Overall Takeaways

Technical SEO is becoming more important, not less.

As AI search evolves, professionals who understand crawling, rendering, indexing, infrastructure, and search mechanics will become increasingly valuable.

The future belongs to SEOs who can combine:

- technical expertise
- experimentation
- data analysis
- critical thinking
- ethical responsibility
